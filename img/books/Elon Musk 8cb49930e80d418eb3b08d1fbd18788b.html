Some of the new words and phrases

  • Mercator projection: Mercator投影是一种常用的航海和地图制作投影方式，它以航海者的航线为设计基础，将地球表面上的经纬线投影为一系列平行纬线和直角的经线，使得地球表面上的航线在地图上呈现为直线。但是，这种投影方式会导致极地附近的地区出现严重的形变，这是其缺点之一。
  • Mayhem
    • Mayhem refers to violent or chaotic events, often involving destruction and disorder. It can also refer to a state of intense confusion or disorder. The term is often used in a dramatic or hyperbolic way to describe a particularly chaotic or destructive situation.
  • apotheosis
    • Apotheosis refers to the elevation or exaltation of a person to the rank of a god. It can also refer to the highest point of development or achievement, or the culmination or climax of something.
  • Foible
    • A foible is a minor weakness or eccentricity in someone's character. It is a quirk or idiosyncrasy that is seen as endearing or amusing rather than a serious flaw.
  • The Heinlein Prize
    • The Heinlein Prize was an award given to individuals who have made significant contributions to space exploration. It was established in 2003 by the estate of science fiction author Robert A. Heinlein, and was awarded every two years until it was discontinued in 2015. The prize was intended to encourage the progress of space exploration and to inspire young people to pursue careers in science and engineering. Recipients of the prize included Burt Rutan, Elon Musk, and Peter Diamandis.

  • chicanery
    • Chicanery refers to the use of deception or trickery, often for personal gain or to achieve a specific goal. It can also refer to the use of legal or political maneuvering to achieve an end, often through misdirection or manipulation.
  • prevail
    • The definition of "prevail" means to prove more powerful or superior, to be victorious, or to be widespread or current in a particular area or at a particular time.
  • infinitestimal
    • Infinitesimal refers to something that is extremely small or almost negligible in size or quantity. It is often used in mathematics to describe a quantity that approaches zero as a limit.
  • Raison d’être
    • Raison d'être is a French phrase that translates to "reason for being" in English. It refers to the fundamental purpose or reason for something's existence or the driving force behind its actions or decisions.
